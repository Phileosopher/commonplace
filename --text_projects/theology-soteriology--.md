
Christians (those truly “saved to begin with”) can:
1. Depart from the faith (1 Timothy 4:1)
2. Wander away from the faith (1 Timothy 6:10)
3. Swerve from the faith (1 Timothy 6:21)
4. Abandon our faith (1 Timothy 5:12)
5. Make shipwreck of our faith (1 Timothy 1:19)
6. Fall from grace (Galatians 5:4)
7. Desert him who called you and turn to a different gospel (Galatians 1:6)
8. Receive the grace of God in vain (2 Corinthians 6:1)
9. Swerve from the truth, upsetting the faith of some (2 Timothy 2:18)
10. Drift away from the truth (Hebrews 2:1)
11. Have an evil, unbelieving heart, leading us to fall away from the living God (Hebrews 3:12)
12. Be hardened by the deceitfulness of sin (Hebrews 3:12-14)
13. Fail to reach the promise of entering into His rest (Hebrews 4:1)
14. Fall by disobedience to enter His rest (Hebrews 4:11)
15. Fall away and become impossible to restore unto repentance (Hebrews 6:4-6)
16. Deliberately go on sinning and no longer have a sacrifice for sins (Hebrews 10:26-27)
17. Shrink back (Hebrews 10:38)
18. Fail to obtain the grace of God (Hebrews 12:15)
19. Wander from the truth (James 5:19-20)
20. Forsake the right way (2 Peter 2:15)
21. Become entangled and overcome AFTER having escaped them (2 Peter 2:20-21)
22. Be carried away with the error of lawless people and lose our stability (2 Peter 3:17)
23. Commit a sin leading to death (1 John 5:16)
24. Stop abiding in the teaching of Christ (2 John 1:9)
25. Be taken out of the book of life (Revelation 22:19)
26. Be not forgiven by the Father (Matthew 6:14-15)
27. Be thrown in with the unbelievers (Luke 12:46)
28. Perform miracles and still be rejected by Jesus (Matthew 7:21-23)
29. Be condemned for not walking in the Spirit (Romans 8:1, 8:13)
30. Be spewed out by Jesus for being lukewarm (Revelation 3:15-17)
31. Have faith that can be nullified through lack of love (1 Corinthians 13:2)
32. Be cut off for not continuing in His kindness (Romans 11:20-22)
33. Be defiled and lose inheritance through acts of immorality (Ephesians 5:5-6)
34. Be deceived and fall under judgment due to sinful conduct (1 Corinthians 6:9-10, Galatians 5:19-21)
35. Have one’s “lampstand removed” (position of light/witness) due to unrepentance (Revelation 2:5)
36. Face the same punishment as hypocrites (unbelievers) if unfaithful (Matthew 24:48-51)
37. Lose reward and inheritance in God’s kingdom due to idolatry, sexual immorality, etc. (Colossians 3:5-6)
38. Be cut down like an unfruitful tree if failing to bear fruit (Luke 13:6-9)
39. Fail to endure and be denied by Christ (2 Timothy 2:12-13)
40. Receive a “part” with unbelievers if living in opposition to Christ’s teachings (Luke 12:46)
41. Fall asleep spiritually and not remain prepared for Christ’s return (Matthew 25:1-13, Parable of the Ten Virgins)
42. Be removed from the Olive Tree if not faithful (symbolizing removal from Christ) (Romans 11:17-21)
43. Face destruction as a consequence of following false prophets or destructive teachings (2 Peter 2:1-3)
44. Be led astray and hardened by the deceitfulness of sin, even after coming to the knowledge of the truth (Hebrews 3:13-14)
45. Possess a faith without works, which James describes as “dead” (James 2:17, 26)
46. Become unfit for the kingdom of God if looking back after putting a hand to the plow (Luke 9:62)
47. Be rejected by the Father if ashamed of Christ before others (Mark 8:38, Luke 9:26)
48. Turn back and no longer walk with Jesus, as seen in the case of some disciples (John 6:66)
49. Receive temporary joy but fall away in times of testing, as illustrated in the Parable of the Sower (Luke 8:13)
50. Find oneself “outside” among the fearful, unbelieving, and abominable, etc. (Revelation 21:8, 22:15)
