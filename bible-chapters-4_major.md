
# Bible chapter summary 4: Major prophets

## Isaiah

1. Judah is rebellious, the offerings are meaningless, Zion is corrupt (2 Chronicles 26-28)
2. the mountain of the Lord's house, the day of reckoning
3. judgment on Jerusalem and Judah, a warning to Zion's daughters
4. a remnant in Zion
5. the song of the vineyard, woes to the wicked
6. Isaiah's commission
7. a message to Ahaz, the sign of Immanuel, judgment to come (2 Chronicles 28)
8. Assyrian invasion prophesied, a call to fear God, judgment to come
9. unto us a child is born, judgment against Israel's pride, hypocrisy, and unrepentance
10. woe to tyrants, judgment on Assyria, a remnant shall return
11. the root of Jesse
12. in that day you will thank God
13. the burden against Babylon
14. restoration for Israel, the fall of Babylon, Assyria, and Philistia
15. the burden against Moab
16. Moab's destruction
17. the burden against Damascus
18. a message to Cush
19. the burden against Egypt, a blessing on the earth
20. a sign against Egypt and Cush
21. Babylon is fallen, the burden against Edom, the burden against Arabia
22. the valley of vision, a message for Shebna
23. the burden against Tyre
24. God's judgment on the earth
25. praise to the victorious God
26. a song of salvation
27. God will destroy Leviathan and save His vineyard
28. the captivity of Ephraim, a cornerstone in Zion, listen and hear
29. woe to David's city, sanctification for the godly
30. the worthless treaty with Egypt, God will be gracious
31. woe to those who rely on Egypt
32. a righteous king, the women of Jerusalem
33. the Lord is exalted
34. judgment on the nations, judgment on Edom
35. the glory of Zion
36. Sennacherib from Assyria threatens Jerusalem (2 Kings 18:13–37, 2 Chronicles 32:1–8)
37. Hezekiah sends for Isaiah who sends a message of deliverance, Sennacherib sends a blasphemous letter, Hezekiah's prayer, Isaiah prophesies Sennacherib's fall, 185,000 Assyrians killed by God that night (2 Kings 19)
38. Hezekiah's illness and recovery, Hezekiah's song of thanksgiving (2 Kings 20:1–11; 2 Chronicles 32:24–31)
39. Hezekiah shows his treasures to Babylon (2 Kings 20:12-19)
40. prepare the way for the Lord, the word of God stands forever, here is your God
41. God's help to Israel, meaningless idols
42. here is my servant, a new song of praise, Israel is deaf adn blind
43. Israel's only savior, a way in the wilderness, Israel's unfaithfulness
44. the Lord has chosen Israel, Jerusalem will be restored
45. God calls Cyrus (2 Chronicles 36:22-23, Ezra 1:1-4)
46. Babylon's idols and God's will
47. Babylon's humiliation
48. Israel's stubbornness, Israel's deliverance is promised
49. the Servant and Light to the Gentiles
50. Israel's sin, the Servant's obedience
51. Zion's salvation, God's fury is removed
52. deliverance for Jerusalem, the Servant exalted
53. the suffering Servant, a grave is assigned
54. future blessings for Zion
55. invitation to the needy
56. salvation for foreigners, Israel's sinful leaders
57. the blessed death of the righteous, God condemns idolatry, healing for the repentant
58. true fasts and sabbaths
59. sin separates us from God, the covenant of the Redeemer
60. future glory for Zion
61. the year of the Lord's favor
62. Zion's salvation and new name
63. God's vengeance on the nations, God's mercies are remembered, a prayer for mercy
64. a prayer for God's power
65. judgments and promises, a new heaven and new earth
66. "Heaven is My throne", rejoice with Jerusalem

2. Israel has forsaken God, the consequence of Israel's sin, Israel's unfaithfulness
3. the harlot's wages, Judah follows Israel's example, a call to repentance
4. a plea to return, disaster is coming from the north, lamentation for Judah
5. nobody is just, God's judgment proclaimed
6. Jerusalem's final warning, an invasion is coming from the north
7. Jeremiah's message at the temple gate, Judah's idolatry persists, the valley of slaughter
