
# Bible chapter summary 8: the Letters

## Romans

1. Paul greets Rome's saints, unashamed of the Gospel, God's wrath against sin
2. God's righteous judgment, the Jews and the Law
3. God remains faithful, nobody is righteous, righteousness through faith in Christ
4. Abraham justified by faith and received the promise
5. the triumph of faith, Christ's sacrifice for the ungodly, death in Adam and life in Christ
6. dead to sin and alive to God, the payment for sin
7. release from the Law, God's Law is holy, struggling with sin
8. walking by the Spirit, heirs with Christ, future glory, God works in all things, more than conquerors
9. Paul's concern for the Jews, God's sovereign choice, Israel's unbelief
10. the word brings salvation
11. a remnant chosen by grace, the Gentiles' ingrafting, all Israel will be saved, a hymn of praise
12. living sacrifices, love/zeal/hope/hospitality, forgiveness
13. submission to authorities, love fulfills the Law, the day is near
14. the law of liberty and love
15. accept one another, Christ the servant of Jews and Gentiles, Paul the minister to the Gentiles, Paul's travel plans
16. personal greetings and love, avoid divisions, greetings from Paul's fellow workers, doxology
